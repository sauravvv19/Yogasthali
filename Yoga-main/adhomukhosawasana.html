Adho Mukha Svanasana (Downward Facing Dog)

Description

Adho Mukha Svanasana, or Downward Facing Dog, is a foundational yoga pose in which the body forms an inverted V-shape, lengthening the spine while stretching the hamstrings, calves, and shoulders. It strengthens the arms and legs, energizes the body, and is used frequently as a transitional and resting pose in many yoga sequences.

Benefits

  • Strengthens arms, shoulders, and legs.
  • Stretches hamstrings, calves, and spine.
  • Improves blood circulation and energizes the body.
  • Helps relieve stiffness in the back and shoulders.
  • Calms the mind and helps reduce stress and fatigue.

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Start on your hands and knees in a tabletop position with wrists under shoulders and knees under hips.
  2. Tuck your toes under, spread your fingers wide, and press your palms firmly into the mat.
  3. On an exhale, lift your knees away from the floor and push your hips up and back into an inverted V-shape.
  4. Keep your feet hip-width apart, toes pointing forward, and gently press your heels toward the floor.
  5. Lengthen your spine and draw your chest slightly toward your thighs, relaxing your head between your upper arms.
  6. Hold for 5â€“10 steady breaths, then bend your knees and return to tabletop to release.

Precautions

  • Avoid deep weight on the wrists or shoulders if you have injuries in these areas.
  • Those with high blood pressure, glaucoma, or vertigo should practice with guidance.
  • Keep a slight bend in the knees and elbows if hamstrings or shoulders are very tight.

Tips

  • Focus on lengthening the spine more than forcing the heels to touch the floor.
  • Engage your core to support the lower back.
  • Keep your neck relaxed and gaze softly toward your feet or knees.
  • Distribute weight evenly between hands and feet for stability.
