Bhujangasana (Cobra Pose)

Description

Bhujangasana, or Cobra Pose, is a gentle backbend that opens the chest and strengthens the spine. It is commonly practiced as part of Surya Namaskar and helps improve posture and spinal flexibility.

Benefits

  • Strengthens the spine and back muscles
  • Improves posture and flexibility
  • Opens the chest and lungs
  • Reduces stress and fatigue
  • Stimulates abdominal organs

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Lie flat on your stomach with legs extended and feet together.
  2. Place your palms under your shoulders, elbows close to the body.
  3. Inhale and gently lift your chest by straightening your arms partially.
  4. Keep your pelvis and legs grounded on the mat.
  5. Roll your shoulders back and lift your chest upward.
  6. Hold the pose for 15â€“30 seconds while breathing normally.

Precautions

  • Avoid if you have recent back injury or spinal surgery.
  • Pregnant women should consult a doctor before practicing.
  • Do not overstretch the lower back.

Tips

  • Keep elbows slightly bent to avoid strain.
  • Focus on lifting the chest rather than pushing with hands.
  • Maintain steady breathing throughout the pose.
